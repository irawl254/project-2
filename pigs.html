Pig Island

Pig Island, officially known as Big Major Cay, is an uninhabited island in the Exuma Cays of the Bahamas, famous for its colony of swimming pigs. These pigs have become a major tourist attraction, drawing visitors from around the world who want to see and swim with them in the crystal-clear waters.

The origin of these pigs remains a mystery, but there are several popular theories. One story suggests that sailors left the pigs on the island with the intention of returning to cook them, but they never came back. Another legend claims that the pigs survived a shipwreck and swam to shore. However, the most plausible explanation is that locals introduced the pigs to the island as a sustainable food source but later abandoned them. Over time, the pigs adapted to their environment and became a unique feature of the island.
