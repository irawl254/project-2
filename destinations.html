Carhenge

Carhenge is a quirky roadside attraction near Alliance, Nebraska, inspired by England’s Stonehenge. Built in 1987 by artist Jim Reinders, it features 39 vintage American cars arranged in the same formation as the original Stonehenge.

Pig Beach

Big Major Cay, also known as Pig Beach, is a famous island in the Bahamas where wild pigs swim freely in the crystal-clear waters. The friendly pigs are believed to have been left by sailors or survived a shipwreck. Visitors can swim with and feed the pigs, making it a unique attraction.

Easter Island

Easter Island, located in the Pacific Ocean, is known for its nearly 1,000 moai statues, created by the Rapa Nui people. These massive stone figures, carved from volcanic rock, represent ancestors and hold cultural significance. The island’s remote location and mysterious history make it a captivating site for researchers and travelers.
