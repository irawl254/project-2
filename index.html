Carhenge

Carhenge is a quirky and fascinating roadside attraction located near Alliance, Nebraska. It’s a replica of England’s famous Stonehenge, but instead of ancient stones, it’s constructed from vintage American cars. The structure was created in 1987 by Jim Reinders as a tribute to his father. Reinders, who had studied Stonehenge’s design while living in England, used 39 cars to replicate the positions and proportions of the original monument.

The cars are painted gray to mimic the look of the stone pillars, and they’re arranged in a circle with some vehicles buried nose-first into the ground and others welded on top to form arches. Carhenge has become a symbol of Americana, blending art, humor, and a nod to British history with the automotive culture of the United States.

In addition to the main monument, the surrounding area, known as the Car Art Reserve, features other car-themed sculptures created by various artists. Carhenge is free to visit and draws tourists looking for offbeat attractions on road trips across the Great Plains
